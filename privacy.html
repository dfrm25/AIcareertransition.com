Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

1. Who We Are

AI Career Transition (aicareertransition.com) is an independent educational website helping professionals learn AI skills. We do not sell products, collect payment information, or require account creation.

2. Information We Collect

We collect minimal data:

  • Usage data via Google Analytics: Page views, time on site, referral source, browser type. This data is aggregated and anonymous. Google Analytics collects this through cookies.
  • Form submissions (Feedback page): If you voluntarily submit feedback, your name, email, and message are stored in a Google Sheet accessible only to the site owner.
  • Browser storage (quiz & saved prompts): Your quiz results and saved prompts are stored only in your own browser and are never sent to our servers.

3. Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Analytics (Google Analytics): To understand how visitors use the site so we can improve content.
  • Advertising (Google AdSense): We display ads served by Google AdSense. Google may use cookies to show personalized ads based on your browsing history. You can opt out via Google's Ad Settings or the NAI opt-out tool.

4. Google AdSense

This site participates in Google AdSense, a third-party advertising program. Google uses cookies (including the DoubleClick cookie) to serve ads based on your prior visits to this and other websites. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to our site and/or other sites on the Internet. You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings.

5. Third-Party Links

Our site links to external tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.) and YouTube videos. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those third-party sites. Please review their privacy policies directly.

6. Data Retention

Form submission data is retained for up to 12 months and used solely for improving the site. Analytics data is retained according to Google's standard retention policies. You may request deletion of your form submission data by contacting us via the Feedback page.

7. Children's Privacy

This site is intended for professional adults and is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Your Rights (GDPR / CCPA)

If you are located in the EU, UK, or California, you have the right to:

  • Access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you
  • Object to processing of your data
  • Request data portability
  • Opt out of personalized advertising (see Section 4)

To exercise these rights, contact us via the Feedback page.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "last updated" date at the top of this page reflects when changes were made. Continued use of the site after any update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

10. Contact

Questions about this Privacy Policy? Use the Contact form on our Feedback page.
